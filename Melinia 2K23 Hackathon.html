What is Health? 

The World Health Organization defines health as “a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity”.
Yet, often we see mental health not being given priority in one’s life.




Factors Stopping Access to Mental Health Treatment: 

1. The stigma surrounding mental health issues- Due to popular fiction and media, the nuances of mental health disorders are often lost. Mental illnesses may be simultaneously glamourised and 
demonized in today’s media. 
(See: Stereotypes about Mental Illness or 13 Reasons Why)
2. Extreme self-reliance- From birth we are taught to be self-reliant. However, sometimes we need to accept that itis okay to need help.
3. Stereotypes regarding therapy and treatment- Therapists and other mental health professional get a bad rap in the media. They are often portrayed as insensitive or eccentric. Or they exacerbate the 
problem. Sometimes the therapy scene is played only for laughs or plot progress. 
(See: Stereotypes about Therapy)
4. Unavailability of mental health professionals- Maybe the person lives in an area with less mental health professionals. Or often minors are unable to seek help due to familial pressure.

How to Maintain Good Mental Health? 

1.	Social Interaction- Humans are social beings. It is important for us to stay connected with those close to us. So, talk to and meet your friends, family and loved ones regularly.
(See: Benefits of Strong Relationships).
2.	Be physically active- Regular exercise has shown to improve mental health.
(See: Physical Activity & Exercise).
3.	Take time for your hobbies- It is important for one to recharge and rewind mentally. A good way to do this is to develop and nurture a hobby for leisure.
4.	Know your limits/trigger- Identifying what upsets or stresses you can make you better equipped to handle such things.
5.	Avoid substances such as drugs and alcohol- They only serve to exacerbate existing mental health issues. 
(See: Effect of Drugs and Alcohol on Mental Health).
6.	Seek professional help when you are struggling to cope- There should be no shame in asking for help when required. In fact, it takes a lot of courage to admit you have a problem. And this is the first step 
to finding a solution. 
(See: Psychotherapy).

FAQs 

1.	What is mental health?
The World Health Organization (WHO) conceptualizes mental health as a “state of well-being in which the individual realizes his or her own abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work 
productively and fruitfully, and is able to make a contribution to his or her community”.

2.	What do I do if I feel like working with a mental health professional is not working?
First try to identify what issues you have with them. Communicating these issues may be helpful. Or else it is advisable to switch to someone else.

3.	Can mental illness be prevented?
Not always. But seeking help early prevents it from becoming more severe.

4.	Can all mental illnesses be cured?
Many mental illnesses are fully curable for certain patients. However, this is not always the case. In such scenarios, there may be ways to treat the illness such that becomes more manageable.
5.	When should I seek a professional’s opinion?
When it becomes to overwhelming for you and your mental state is affecting your ability to function daily.

6.	Do all mental illnesses require medicines to treat them?
No, all patients may not require medicines but some do.

7.	Do child experience mental health issues?
Yes, people of any age group can experience mental health issues.

8.	Are all mentally ill people violent and unpredictable?
No, only a small percentage of mentally ill people are violent. People with mental illnesses are more vulnerable to be victims of a violent crime.

9.	What are some factors that contribute to mental health problems?
Genes, physical illness, injury, brain chemistry, trauma or a history of abuse. However, people may develop a mental illness without these pre-existing factors.
